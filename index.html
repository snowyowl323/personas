Part 0: Overview

In this project, I analyzed an interface in a public space. The interface that I chose is the soft serve ice cream machine in my college cafeteria. Next, I created questions to ask people about their experience with the interface and to better understand their interaction in getting ice cream. Using the my observations of three users as well as their responses to my questions, I made two personas for people who use the soft serve machine in the cafeteria. Finally, I created a storyboard to document a personas interaction with the interface.

Part 1: Preparation

For this project, I chose to analyze the use of the soft serve ice cream machine in my college cafeteria. The soft serve machine experience involves grabbing a bowl, plate, or cone, placing container under the machine, and pulling a lever to dispense ice cream. Then, once the container is full to the user's satisfaction, they can choose to add sprinkles or crushed oreos on top from a station to the left of the machine. Next, I created some questions to ask users about their experience with the soft serve machine:

  1. On a scale of 1 to 10, where 1 is easy and 10 is diffult, how easy or difficult was it to use the ice cream machine?
  2. Have you used an ice cream machine before? (If yes) How often do you use a machine? (If no) How did you figure out how to use the machine?
  3. What did you like most about the ice cream machine experience?
  4. What was your least favorite part of using the ice cream machine experience?
  5. How much time would you be willing to spend to get ice cream?

This is the soft serve ice cream machine in the dining hall. The plates and bowls are where users can get a container to put their ice cream in. The cones can also be used to hold ice cream. The ice cream dispensers are where the ice cream comes out of, and the container is held underneath. The levers are pulled down to dispense the ice cream. Finally, the toppings can be added on top of the ice cream.

Summary of Interviews

After observing users interact with the interface, I asked them the questions I created in Part 1. I summarized their responses to the questions in the following bullet points:

  • All of the users I interviewed found the soft serve ice cream machine easy to use.
  • One user used the ice cream machine regularly (multiple times a week), while the other two almost never used it.
  • Their favorite part of the machine varied, with respondents liking the toppings or the anticipation of being able to eat ice cream. One respondent noted that it was helpful that you could dispense ice cream more slowly by pulling the lever part of the way down.
  • Their least favorite parts were the pressure of having to use the machine in front of other people and swirling the ice cream correctly.
  • Everyone agreed that they wouldn't want to spend a lot of time getting ice cream, with respondents saying that they wouldn't want to spend more than a minute or two getting ice cream.

Part 3: Personas

For the next part of the project, I will be creating two personas based on the user interviews. Using these personas, I will create empathy maps which show what the person thinks, feels, says, and does. One persona, Ethan, uses the ice cream machine regularly, while the other persona, Olivia, uses the ice cream machine much less frequently.

Ethan

Ethan is a college student who is on the college meal plan and who loves desserts. He stops by the dessert station at most meals.

Ethan faces the problem of trying to get the most ice cream in the least amount of time and with the least hassle. He adds toppings to his ice cream for variety. He tries to go get ice cream when the line is shortest, but will go even if the line is a few minutes. Ethan represents the users who got ice cream often but are more self concious/shy.

Thinks

  • I don't think the line will get any shorter than it is right now.
  • The line does look kind of long.
  • I wonder if I want ice cream or another dessert?

Feels

  • Pressure to move quickly when there is a line behind him.
  • Awkward when he is the center of attention.

Says

  • Sorry I'm taking so long.
  • Excuse me, can I get to the toppings please?

Does

  • Gets his ice cream quickly by pulling the lever all the way down.
  • Adds toppings to his ice cream.
  • Uses bowl or cup to maximize amount of ice cream he can get.
  • Goes to get ice cream even when the line is a little longer.

Olivia

Olivia is also a college student who is on the college meal plan who gets desserts occasionally.

The biggest trouble she has with the interface is getting the ice cream to swirl in a cone correctly. She pulls the lever down carefully to dispense the ice cream slowly, and doesn't add toppings to her ice cream. Olivia won't get ice cream if there is a line. Olivia represents users by not using the ice cream machine frequently, using a cone, and being worried about the quality of her swirl.

Thinks

  • I'm excited to get ice cream!
  • Does anyone else want to go get ice cream with me?
  • The line is long, and I don't want to wait in line for ice cream.

Feels

  • Excited to eat ice cream cone.
  • Worried about messing up the swirl on the ice cream cone.
  • Doubts themselves more often.

Says

  • Is this how you do swirl the ice cream?
  • This didn't turn out very well.

Does

  • Pulls the lever part of the way down to swirl the ice cream meticulously.
  • Doesn't add toppings to the ice cream.
  • Uses a cone to eat the ice cream.
  • Only goes when the ice cream line is short/non-existant.

Part 4: Storyboarding

Using the Ethan persona that I created in Part 3, I made a storyboard for a possible interaction of his with the cafeteria soft serve ice cream machine.

Storyboard Frames

1. Ethan Gets in Line

Ethan just finished his dinner at the college cafeteria. He wants something sweet, and today he decides he wants ice cream. He looks at the ice cream machine line and even though there are a few people, he decides to get in line for ice cream.

2. Ethan Grabs Bowl

While Ethan is waiting in line for ice cream, he grabs a bowl from under the counter. He prefers bowls over cones because they are better for toppings.

3. Ethan Fills Bowl

Once he gets to the front of the line, Ethan uses his right hand to hold the bowl under the machine's ice cream dispenser.

4. Ethan Pulls Lever

While he holds the bowl with his right hand, Ethan uses his left hand to pull the lever while he moves his right hand in a circle to create vague swirling motion. Ethan feels rushed by the people behind him in line so he tries to fill the bowl as quickly as possible.

5. Ethan Gets Toppings

Ethan moves out of line, and then asks if he can squeeze through line to get to the toppings station. He heaps crumbled oreos into his bowl.

6. Ethan Returns to Seat

Finally, Ethan takes the ice cream bowl he has created and heads back to his table to eat his dessert.
